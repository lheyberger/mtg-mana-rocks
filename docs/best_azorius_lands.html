Best Azorius lands

Top 10 mana fixing Azorius lands for your Commander deck

Building a 2 colors Commander deck is always tricky. Compared to the other configurations (1 color and 3+ colors), 2 colors decks have less ways of fixing their manabase than other combinations.

That's why it's particularly important to know what your options are if you want to make the most out of your manabase.

10. Port Town

More restrictive than Glacial Fortress because it requires you to have an actual Plains or an Island in your starting hand.

9. Glacial Fortress

More flexible than Port Town because you can keep a hand with a fetch land in addition to Glacial Fortress.

8. Skycloud Expanse

You cannot keep your hand if this is your only land, other than that, you are good to go.

7. Hengegate Pathway // Mistgate Pathway

Doesn't really fix your mana but at least you get to choose your color.

6. Mystic Gate

Instant speed fixing for both of your colors if you happen to already have one of those two colors. Provides you colorless mana in the meantime.

5. Adarkar Wastes

Instant speed fixing for your deck with a 1 life drawback each time. Won't hurt you if you don't need the colored mana.

4. Sea of Clouds

No drawback in a Commander game other than not being fetchable.

3. Hallowed Fountain

Almost as good as it's older brother, Tundra.

Prefered target when cracking an Flooded Strand if you have no relevant play to follow with.

2. Tundra

The king of dual lands: Perfect, no drawback, no tempo loss.

I only ranked Flooded Strand higher because of the amazing flexibility.

1. Flooded Strand

This is the perfect land for your Azorius Commander deck.

It's a Plains when you need it. It's an Island when you need it. If your budget allows it, it can be a Tundra at instant speed. If you have nothing else to play, it can be a Hallowed Fountain that enters the battlefield tapped.

You should always wait until the last minute to crack your fetch land: if you need to play Swords to Plowshares or Dispel at instant speed, just go fetch a Tundra, otherwise, at the end of your opponent's turn, go fetch a Hallowed Fountain and let it enter the battlefield tapped.

Cracking a fetch land and shuffling your library is also what makes it so good. If for whatever reason, you're not happy with your scry, or the cards you put back on top of your library with a Scroll Rack or Brainstorm, you can get rid of them by cracking the Flooded Strand.

Fetch lands are also good targets for recursion spells or abilities like Brought Back, Sevinne's Reclamation or Sun Titan.

You can also double your fetch land activations with cards like Rings of Brighthearth.
