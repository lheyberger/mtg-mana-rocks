Best Izzet lands

Top 10 mana fixing Izzet lands for your Commander deck

Building a 2 colors Commander deck is always tricky. Compared to the other configurations (1 color and 3+ colors), 2 colors decks have less ways of fixing their manabase than other combinations.

That's why it's particularly important to know what your options are if you want to make the most out of your manabase.

10. Frostboil Snarl

More restrictive than Sulfur Falls because it requires you to have an actual Island or a Mountain in your starting hand.

9. Sulfur Falls

More flexible than Frostboil Snarl because you can keep a hand with a fetch land in addition to Sulfur Falls.

8. Riverglide Pathway // Lavaglide Pathway

Doesn't really fix your mana but at least you get to choose your color.

7. Cascade Bluffs

Instant speed fixing for both of your colors if you happen to already have one of those two colors. Provides you colorless mana in the meantime.

6. Shivan Reef

Instant speed fixing for your deck with a 1 life drawback each time. Won't hurt you if you don't need the colored mana.

5. Fiery Islet

Instant speed fixing for your deck with a 1 life drawback each time. Can replace itself if you are desperate to draw a card.

4. Training Center

No drawback in a Commander game other than not being fetchable.

3. Steam Vents

Almost as good as it's older brother, Volcanic Island.

Prefered target when cracking a Scalding Tarn if you have no relevant play to follow with.

2. Volcanic Island

The king of dual lands: Perfect, no drawback, no tempo loss.

I only ranked Scalding Tarn higher because of the amazing flexibility.

1. Scalding Tarn

This is the perfect land for your Izzet Commander deck.

It's an Island when you need it. It's a Mountain when you need it. If your budget allows it, it can be a Volcanic Island at instant speed. If you have nothing else to play, it can be a Steam Vents that enters the battlefield tapped.

You should always wait until the last minute to crack your fetch land: if you need to play Dispel at instant speed or a Pyroblast at the end of your opponent's turn, just go fetch a Volcanic Island, otherwise, at the end of your opponent's turn, go fetch a Steam Vents and let it enter the battlefield tapped.

Cracking a fetch land and shuffling your library is also what makes it so good. If for whatever reason, you're not happy with your scry, or the cards you put back on top of your library with a Scroll Rack or Brainstorm, you can get rid of them by cracking the Scalding Tarn.

You can also double your fetch land activations with cards like Rings of Brighthearth.
