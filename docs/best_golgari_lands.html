Best Golgari lands

Top 10 mana fixing Golgari lands for your Commander deck

Building a 2 colors Commander deck is always tricky. Compared to the other configurations (1 color and 3+ colors), 2 colors decks have less ways of fixing their manabase than other combinations.

That's why it's particularly important to know what your options are if you want to make the most out of your manabase.

10. Woodland Cemetery

You cannot keep your hand if this is your only land, other than that, you are good to go.

9. Tainted Wood

Will shine more in decks relying heavily on Black. Will provide colorless mana in the meantime.

8. Darkbore Pathway // Slitherbore Pathway

Doesn't really fix your mana but at least you get to choose your color.

7. Twilight Mire

Instant speed fixing for both of your colors if you happen to already have one of those two colors. Provides you colorless mana in the meantime.

6. Llanowar Wastes

Instant speed fixing for your deck with a 1 life drawback each time. Won't hurt you if you don't need the colored mana.

5. Nurturing Peatland

Instant speed fixing for your deck with a 1 life drawback each time. Can replace itself if you are desperate to draw a card.

4. Undergrowth Stadium

No drawback in a Commander game other than not being fetchable.

3. Overgrown Tomb

Almost as good as it's older brother, Bayou.

Prefered target when cracking an Verdant Catacombs if you have no relevant play to follow with.

2. Bayou

The king of dual lands: Perfect, no drawback, no tempo loss.

I only ranked Verdant Catacombs higher because of the amazing flexibility.

1. Verdant Catacombs

This is the perfect land for your Golgari Commander deck.

It's a Swamp when you need it. It's an Forest when you need it. If your budget allows it, it can be a Bayou at instant speed. If you have nothing else to play, it can be a Overgrown Tomb that enters the battlefield tapped.

You should always wait until the last minute to crack your fetch land: if you need to play Vampiric Tutor or Worldly Tutor at instant speed, just go fetch a Bayou, otherwise, at the end of your opponent's turn, go fetch a Overgrown Tomb and let it enter the battlefield tapped.

Cracking a fetch land and shuffling your library is also what makes it so good. If for whatever reason, you're not happy with your scry, or the cards you put back on top of your library with a Scroll Rack or Sylvan Library, you can get rid of them by cracking the Verdant Catacombs.

Fetch lands are also good targets for recursion spells or abilities like Crucible of Worlds.

You can also double your fetch land activations with cards like Rings of Brighthearth.
