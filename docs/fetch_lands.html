Fetch Lands

Quick guide on how to improve your EDH manabase

With the exception of monocolor commanders, properly building your manabase can be a real challenge.

The first candidates you should consider when selecting your lands are fetches.

History

Fetch lands were first introduced in Mirage as an allied color cycle. Each of them enters the battlefield tapped and can be sacrificed to search for a land card with at least one of two basic land types of an allied color pair.

The ones we are really interested in were first introduced in Onslaught, again in allied colors only, but the full cycle was completed in Zendikar with the addition of the enemy colors.

Each of these lands enter the battlefield untapped. Paying one life and sacrificing them allows their controller to search their library for a land card with at least one of two basic land types.

Why should I consider them?

Fetch lands are the perfect lands for your multicolor Commander decks:

  • They can be any basic land when you need it.
  • If your budget allows it, they can be any dual land at instant speed.
  • If you don't have a relevant play to follow up, they can be any shock land that enters the battlefield tapped.
  • They enable some recursion shenanigans with cards like Brought Back or Sun Titan.
  • You can double the effect of their activations with cards like Rings of Brighthearth.

Cracking fetch lands and shuffling your library is also what makes them so good. If for whatever reason, you're not happy with cards you just put back on top of your library with a Scroll Rack or Brainstorm, you can get rid of them by fetching and shuffling your library.

In addition of being very good in almost all commander decks, fetch lands integrates very well in a few archetypes:

  • Landfall, with commanders like Tatyova, Benthic Druid
  • Land Matters, with commanders like Omnath, Locus of Rage, Omnath, Locus of the Roil and Lord Windgrace
  • Recursion, with commanders like Muldrotha, the Gravetide
